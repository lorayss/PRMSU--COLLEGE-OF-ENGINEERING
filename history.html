About the COE Departments

Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering

The Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering Program was first proposed to be offered in Botolan Campus under the Self-Liquidating Program Scheme in early 2002 under the leadership of President Feliciano S. Rosete. The RMTU Board of Regents approved the proposal in early 2003, but Iba Campus was chosen as its venue due to facilities and faculty availability.

BSCE had its first batch graduate in March 2008 with a seventy percent passing rate in the board examination. In 2010, the program was transferred to CEA as a regular program. The BSCE received its Certificate of Program Compliance and Level I AACCUP accreditation in 2016 and Level II in 2019.

Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering began in the 2nd Semester of AY 2001-2002, approved as a self-liquidating program under the Institute of Evening Opportunity Program (IEOP). The first Program Chair was Engr. Juvelyn M. Bueno, with the first batch graduating in 2007.

Over time, the program expanded to morning and evening sessions under the College of Communications and Information Technology (CCIT) and IEOP. In 2018, the program was transferred under the College of Engineering (COE) in compliance with CHED recommendations.

Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering

The Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering began when the RMPC Board of Regents approved its curriculum. The program graduated its first batch of students in 2001, making it one of the oldest programs in the College, initially headed by Engr. Simon Jaring.

Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering

The Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering started with the RMPC Board of Regents approving its curriculum. The program graduated its first batch of students in 2001 and is also among the oldest programs in the College. The department was initially headed by Engr. Noel M. Dioyan.
